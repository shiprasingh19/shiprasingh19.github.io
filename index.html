About

I am a forest socio-ecological systems researcher studying how ecosystems function, how people value and depend on them, and how restoration can be planned in ways that are ecologically strong, socially fair, and climate resilient.

My research integrates field-based forest inventory, functional trait measurements, stakeholder perception data, and ecosystem service assessment to understand how ecological change shapes forest-resource access, community wellbeing, and restoration priorities in mountain systems.

Research

My research combines forest ecology, plant functional traits, ecosystem services, stakeholder knowledge, and spatial approaches to understand and restore mountain forests.

Himalayan Forest Ecosystem Services

Assessment of provisioning, regulating, cultural, and supporting services across Himalayan forest landscapes and elevation gradients.

Plant Functional Traits

Trait-based analysis of forest composition, ecosystem functioning, disturbance response, species regeneration, and restoration potential.

Equity and Livelihoods

Understanding how forest resources are accessed, used, valued, and distributed among mountain communities and stakeholder groups.

Restoration and Climate Resilience

Developing trait-informed and livelihood-sensitive frameworks for forest restoration, climate adaptation, and sustainable landscape management.

Linking plant functional traits, ecosystem services, and equitable forest restoration

I study Himalayan forest social-ecological systems, focusing on plant functional traits, forest ecosystem services, stakeholder dependence, livelihood interactions, and equitable access to forest resources under environmental change.

Explore my research

Research

My research integrates forest ecology, plant functional traits, ecosystem service assessment, stakeholder valuation, and social-ecological systems approaches to understand how mountain forests support biodiversity, livelihoods, and restoration planning.

Himalayan Forest Ecosystem Services

Assessment of provisioning, regulating, cultural, and supporting services across Himalayan forest landscapes and elevation gradients.

Plant Functional Traits

Trait-based analysis of forest composition, ecosystem functioning, disturbance response, and restoration potential.

Equity and Livelihoods

Understanding how forest resources are accessed, used, valued, and distributed among mountain communities.

Restoration and Conservation Planning

Developing trait-informed and livelihood-sensitive frameworks for forest restoration and sustainable landscape management.

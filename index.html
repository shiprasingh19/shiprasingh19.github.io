Forest Socio-Ecological Systems | Himalayan Ecosystems

Linking plant functional traits, ecosystem services, and equitable forest restoration

I study how Himalayan forests function, how people value and depend on them, and how restoration can support biodiversity, livelihoods, and climate resilience.

Research Focus

My work connects forest ecology, plant functional traits, ecosystem services, stakeholder knowledge, and restoration planning in Himalayan mountain landscapes.

Forest Ecosystem Services

Understanding how Himalayan forests provide ecological, cultural, and livelihood benefits across environmental gradients.

Plant Functional Traits

Using leaf, stem, root, and whole-plant traits to assess ecosystem functioning, regeneration, and restoration potential.

Equity and Livelihoods

Studying how forest resources are accessed, valued, and distributed among mountain communities and stakeholder groups.

Restoration and Climate Resilience

Developing trait-informed and socially fair approaches for forest restoration and climate adaptation.

Research

My research focuses on Himalayan forest socio-ecological systems, linking plant functional traits, forest structure, ecosystem services, stakeholder dependence, and restoration planning across environmental gradients.

Himalayan Forest Ecosystem Services

I assess provisioning, regulating, cultural, and supporting ecosystem services across Himalayan forest landscapes. This work connects ecological supply, human demand, stakeholder values, and forest-resource dependence.

Plant Functional Traits and Forest Functioning

I use leaf, stem, root, and whole-plant functional traits to understand species distribution, regeneration potential, disturbance response, biomass accumulation, and ecosystem functioning in Himalayan forests.

Equity, Access, and Livelihood Dependence

My work examines how forest benefits such as fuelwood, fodder, timber, grazing resources, cultural values, and livelihood-supporting products are accessed, used, and distributed among mountain communities.

Restoration and Climate Resilience

I aim to develop trait-informed and socially fair restoration approaches that support biodiversity, ecosystem services, livelihood security, and climate resilience in mountain landscapes.

Current Research Directions

Forest Ecosystem Services along Himalayan Environmental Gradients

This research integrates forest inventory, plant functional traits, stakeholder surveys, and ecosystem service assessment to understand how Himalayan forests support ecological functioning and community wellbeing across elevation gradients.

Trait-Based Restoration Frameworks for Himalayan Forests

This work explores how species-level functional traits can guide restoration planning, species selection, regeneration assessment, and climate-resilient forest management.

Forest Resource Access, Equity, and Livelihood Security

This direction links ecological data with social and livelihood information to understand how forest structure, disturbance, governance, and social factors shape access to forest benefits among different user groups.

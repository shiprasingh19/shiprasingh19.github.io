About

I am a forest ecologist working on Himalayan ecosystem stability using trait-based approaches, valuing ecosystem services, community dependence, and equitable restoration. My research focuses on how forest ecosystems function, how people value and depend on forest resources, and how restoration can be planned in ways that are ecologically sustainable, socially fair, and climate resilient.

I am a nature lover, and travel keeps my spirit alive. I am deeply drawn to Sanskrit, Indian philosophy, and spirituality, which shape how I reflect on life and nature. I enjoy playing, where silence finds rhythm and thought becomes music.

About

I am a forest socio-ecological systems researcher studying how ecosystems function, how people value and depend on them, and how restoration can be planned in ways that are ecologically strong, socially fair, and climate resilient.

My work focuses on Himalayan forest landscapes, where ecological change, community dependence, biodiversity, and restoration priorities are closely connected. I combine forest inventory, plant functional traits, soil properties, stakeholder surveys, and ecosystem service assessment to understand how forests support both ecological functioning and local livelihoods.

I am especially interested in linking trait-based forest ecology with questions of access, equity, livelihood dependence, and restoration planning. Through this work, I aim to contribute to forest management approaches that are scientifically robust, socially inclusive, and useful for climate-resilient mountain landscapes.

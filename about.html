About

I am a forest ecologist working on Himalayan ecosystem stability using trait-based approaches, ecosystem service valuation, community dependence, and equitable restoration. My research focuses on how forest ecosystems function, how people value and depend on forest resources, and how restoration can be planned in ways that are ecologically sustainable, socially fair, and climate resilient.

I am a nature lover, and travel keeps my spirit wandering. Sanskrit, Indian philosophy, and spirituality are quiet rivers within me, shaping how I see life, nature, and the self. I love to play the sitar, where silence finds rhythm and thought becomes music.
